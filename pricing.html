How outcome pricing works

Baseline first. Verify second. Bill third.

Three steps, in this order, every time. No fee is agreed until the baseline is measured and the outcome is named. Nothing triggers until the result is verified against your own telemetry.

01
Before any fee · the workshop

We measure your baseline.

From your real spend, alert volume, or cycle time. This is the number everything anchors to — agreed jointly, in writing, before work starts.

02
During the pilot · the substrate

We verify the delta against your data.

The 14-day live substrate produces the result on your telemetry — per-PR cost, MTTR, alert reduction. Not a synthetic benchmark. Your dashboards, your numbers.

03
After verification · the invoice

We bill a share of the named outcome.

The fee is a pre-agreed share of the measured result. If the outcome underperforms the baseline, the fee scales down with it. If it doesn't ship, it doesn't trigger.

Common questions

The questions procurement always asks.

What if the outcome underperforms?

The fee scales with the measured result. If the delta comes in below the baseline target, the fee comes down with it. If nothing ships, nothing triggers — you're never paying for effort.

Who verifies the numbers?

Your own telemetry does. The substrate produces the metric on your dashboards — CloudTrail, Grafana, your ITSM. We agree the measurement method in the workshop, before any work starts.

Are we locked in afterward?

No. Knowledge transfer is the deliverable — source, runbooks, evals, and decision logs are yours at handoff. Ongoing ops support is optional, never required.

Does our data leave our cloud?

Never. The platform deploys inside your VPC, on your keys, with your IAM. There's no data exfiltration path — that's a design constraint, not a setting.
