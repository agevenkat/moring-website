01 · The 90-day arc

How four weeks become twelve.

The first four weeks are the workshop. The next eight are the build. Both halves are the same engineering team. You meet them on day one, and they ship your system on day 84.

// stage 01 · by day 28

Strategy. Ready to sign.

  • Ranked shortlist of opportunities. Effort sized, value sized, owner identified.
  • ROI model your finance team can defend in front of the CFO without a follow-up.
  • Architecture sketch the CTO can defend with a CISO and a regulator in the room.
  • Outcome pricing on the top pick. Fixed fee, anchored to a named metric, no hourly billing.
// stage 02 · by day 84

Deploy. In production.

  • The agentic system from Day 28 live in your cloud against real traffic.
  • Cost observability live. Audit lake live. Governance hooks live. FinOps view shared with your team.
  • An engineering case study and the runbook recipe for the next workflow.
  • A handoff plan, on a date you pick, for the partnership that follows the first build.

02 · For each signer

What your three signers each walk away with.

The workshop is sized so each signer leaves with the artifact that lets them say yes. None of the artifacts are a placeholder.

// signer 01

CTO / VP Eng.

An architecture you can defend. The agent boundaries, the orchestration shape, the data plane, the failure modes, the rollback plan. Drawn in your stack, not a generic reference diagram.

// signer 02

CISO / CRO.

Governance shown working. Lineage, evaluation gates, override trail, regulator-ready exports. Live in the demo, not in a slide. The CISO leaves with a list of controls they can point to.

// signer 03

CFO / FinOps.

A defensible ROI. The metric we are anchoring to, the baseline, the model, the savings curve, the timing of the savings. The CFO leaves with a number their auditor will accept.

04 · Common questions

Things people ask before they sign.

If yours isn't here, the meeting button at the top is the fastest way to ask it.

What does the workshop cost?
A $5,000 reference fee covers four weeks of focused work and ten hours of synchronous time with Moring engineering leadership. The fee is credited against the first deploy if you proceed. If you do not proceed, the fee is the only money you spend.
Who attends from our side?
Three signers, typically: a CTO or VP of Engineering, a CISO or CRO, and a CFO or FinOps lead. We want the people who can say yes in the room, not the people who would brief them later.
What do we walk out with on Day 28?
A ranked shortlist of opportunities with effort and value sized, an ROI model your finance team can defend, an architecture sketch the CTO can defend, and outcome pricing on the top pick. Enough to say yes or no with conviction.
What is live by Day 84 if we proceed?
The top pick from Day 28 running in your cloud against real traffic, with cost observability, an audit lake, and governance hooks live. Plus the engineering case study and the recipe for the next workflow.
Do we need to be on AWS?
AWS is our primary cloud and most engagements run there. We support Azure and GCP where there is a real workload need. We will tell you in the workshop if your stack makes us a poor fit.
Do you subcontract any of the delivery?
No. The Moring engineers who scope your workshop are on the team that ships your Day-84 system. Atlanta and Chennai are both Moring employees. No external delivery partners.
Which foundation models do you use?
Anthropic Claude (Sonnet, Opus), OpenAI (GPT-4o, GPT-4o mini), and open-weight families (Llama, Mistral, Cohere). We pick the right model for each step in a workflow. We are not exclusive to any provider.
