Common questions

What SVPs ask before signing.

Who needs to be in the 10-hour workshop?

Three people, ten hours, one room (or one Zoom). The SVP of Data & AI, the CTO or VP of Engineering, and one platform / data leader who knows the systems. Rajarajan runs it. Anyone else slows it down.

Why developer usecases first — not customer-facing?

Two reasons. First: the Moring substrate is purpose-built for the agentic SDLC, so developer usecases give the fastest path to a measured, governed deploy. Second: a developer-usecase win earns you the Year-2 mandate to do the external ones — with the substrate, governance, and case-study recipe already in place.

What happens if we already have an AI strategy?

Even better. We pressure-test it. The 10-hour workshop scores your existing shortlist against impact, feasibility, and governance load — and tells you which ones survive contact with a real architecture and a real CISO.

Can the deploy be on AWS / Azure / GCP?

Yes — the substrate is cloud-agnostic. We're AWS-deepest (Bedrock-native, IAM-aware, marketplace-listed), and we've shipped to Azure and GCP under the same architecture.

How does pricing work?

The 10-hour workshop is fixed-fee. The 4-week strategy phase is fixed-fee. The 12-week deploy is outcome-priced — tied to the metric your shortlist scores define. We tell you the number in week 0; you say yes or no in week 4.

Is this the same as the 14-day AI-DLC pilot?

No. The 14-day pilot is for platform teams who already know the developer usecase they want to govern. This engagement is for SVPs of Data & AI who need to pick the usecase first — across internal, developer, and external — before deploying. Both end with a substrate in your cloud.
