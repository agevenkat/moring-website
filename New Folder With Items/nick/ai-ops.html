Solution 03 · AI Ops · early access

IT operations that self-monitor. Self-triage. Self-heal.

Moring builds AI Ops: an operational brain that sits above your existing observability stack (Dynatrace, ELK, SolarWinds, Oracle EM, your CMDB or Jira) and turns thousands of raw alerts into a handful of actionable incidents. 01 · How we think about AIOps

Three things, not one.

AIOps breaks down into three jobs: detecting that something is wrong, figuring out why, and doing the safe thing about it. Most teams handle the first. The second is harder, and only a few teams reach the third. The third stays hard because of governance, and governance is the part of the Moring substrate we have already built for our other engagements.

// 01

Self-monitor.

Cross-tool ingestion that turns Dynatrace, ELK, SolarWinds, Oracle EM, and your CMDB into a single signal stream. The substrate dedupes, correlates by topology, and surfaces an incident instead of a thousand alerts. Noise drops; the on-call channel gets quiet for the right reasons.

cross-tooltopology aware
// 02

Self-triage.

A topology walker pulls the service graph and a change-log reader pulls recent deploys. A correlator agent posts the probable cause to the incident channel before the human on-call has finished logging in. A runbook lookup is attached.

RCAchange-aware
// 03

Self-heal.

Scoped, reversible runbook execution. The agent runs the recipe that the team has pre-approved for this incident class. Every action is logged, every action is reversible, every action is governed. The blast radius is bounded by the runbook, not by the model.

reversiblerunbook-boundedgoverned

02 · How we ship this

AI-DLC in operations.

Same four pillars. AIOps-specific agent types and reversible-action language.

// pillar 01 · agents

Three Ops agents.

A signal correlator that joins events across tools. A topology walker that traverses the CMDB and service graph to surface probable cause. A runbook executor that runs the pre-approved recipe with full audit. Each one is scoped and replaceable.

signal correlatortopology walkerrunbook executor
// pillar 02 · orchestrations

Reversible by default.

Every action the runbook executor takes is reversible. The orchestration tracks the rollback path the same way it tracks the action path. If the recovery makes things worse, the substrate has the undo step ready.

reversiblerollback-awareHITL gates
// pillar 03 · observability

Action trail.

Every action the agent runs is signed, timestamped, and attributed. Change management and SRE share one trail. The post-mortem is a query against the trail, not a Slack history excavation.

signed actionschange attribution
// pillar 04 · cost

Cost and risk.

The cost lens covers tokens and the risk lens covers blast radius. A runbook that touches a tier-0 service requires a human sign-off, regardless of the model's confidence. The CISO sets the lines, the substrate enforces them.
