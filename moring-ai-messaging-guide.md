## Top-level building blocks (the one-pager)

### Problem
Enterprise AI-assisted coding plateaus at 15–25% gains. Going fully agentic delivers Tier-2 productivity but introduces runaway LLM spend, secret leakage, prompt-injection exposure, and unauditable agent behavior. Most platform teams are stuck choosing between *safe-but-slow* (Tier 1) and *fast-but-ungoverned* (Tier 2). The cost of that stall is measurable in CFO escalations and stalled CISO reviews.

### Product
Moring **AI-DLC** is a governed agentic SDLC substrate that ships to your cloud in 14 days. Five live components — cost observability, governed dev workflow, signed plugin distribution, failure drill, and a real engineering case study — instrument 9 of 11 SDLC stages across one shared substrate.

### Who it's for
Enterprise engineering organizations of 200+ engineers already running an agentic SDLC. The buying committee is the **CTO / VP of Engineering**, with the **CISO** and **CFO / FinOps** as required co-signers. Platform leads are the in-house champion.

### Why it's better
- **Live, in your cloud.** Every component is curl-able, queryable, reproducible. No slides, no synthetic agents, no business-vertical pretense.
- **Same UX, governed underneath.** Engineers keep using whichever agent runtime your platform team already supports. The substrate enforces policy without disrupting workflow.
- **One substrate, five levers.** Cost, productivity, governance, risk, and cycle-time wins all amortize on the same foundation.
- **The demo is the artifact.** After 14 days, the platform team owns the source and the recipe. Year-2 amplifier ships at marginal cost.
